Know more about the team...

Linh, Holiday, and Yanina were three classmates bound by a shared passion: their love for the plastic arts. In their small town, they noticed with sadness how creativity seemed to fade among the youth, consumed by technology and daily routines. One day, while sharing coffee after class, Linh brought up the recent rise in popularity of superheroes thanks to movies and TV shows. Excited, Yanina suggested they could harness that interest to promote art in the community. Holiday chimed in, proposing they focus on comicsâ€”a perfect blend of storytelling and visual arts. Determined to make a difference, the three friends launched QueenComics, a space where kids and adults could explore their creativity through comic creation. They offered workshops on drawing, creative writing, and character design, inspiring participants to create their own superheroes and villains. The project was a hit. The community flocked to the workshops, and soon the walls of the center were adorned with colorful comics and unique characters. Children discovered new ways to express themselves, while adults rekindled forgotten passions. Thanks to Linh, Holiday, and Yanina's initiative, QueenComics not only sparked creativity in their town but also strengthened bonds among its members. The plastic arts found new life, and comics became the perfect medium to tell everyone's stories and dreams. The End.
